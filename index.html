How to read a candlestick chart?

A candlestick chart shows price movement for a specific time period:

  • Body: The rectangle shows the opening and closing prices (green for price up, red for price down).
  • Wicks/Shadows: Thin lines above and below show the highest and lowest prices.
  • Interpretation: Long bodies indicate strong buying/selling pressure; long wicks show price rejection or volatility.

What is a Simple Moving Average (SMA) and how do I use short and long term SMA lines?

A Simple Moving Average (SMA) is a technical analysis indicator that shows the average stock price over a set number of periods, smoothing out short-term fluctuations to reveal the trend.

  • Short-Term SMA (e.g., 10-day or 20-day): Reacts quickly to price changes, useful for identifying short-term trends.
  • Long-Term SMA (e.g., 50-day or 200-day): Reacts slower, helpful for spotting long-term trends.
  • Usage Tip: When the short-term SMA crosses above the long-term SMA, it may signal a potential uptrend ("Golden Cross"); when it crosses below, it may indicate a downtrend ("Death Cross").

What is the Relative Strength Index (RSI) and how is it used?

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale from 0 to 100.

  • Overbought: RSI above 70 may indicate the stock is overvalued and could face a pullback.
  • Oversold: RSI below 30 may indicate the stock is undervalued and could rebound.
  • Usage Tip: RSI works best with other indicators to confirm buy/sell signals.

What is the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) and how is it used?

The MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a stock's price.

  • MACD Line: Difference between the 12-day EMA and 26-day EMA.
  • Signal Line: 9-day EMA of the MACD line, used to trigger buy/sell signals.
  • Usage Tip: When the MACD crosses above the signal line, it can indicate a buy signal; crossing below can indicate a sell signal.

What is the Exponential Moving Average (EMA) and how is it different from SMA?

The Exponential Moving Average (EMA) is similar to the SMA but gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new data.

  • Faster Response: EMA reacts more quickly to price changes than SMA.
  • Short-Term EMA: Commonly used for quick trend identification (e.g., 12-day EMA).
  • Usage Tip: Crossovers between short-term and long-term EMAs can indicate potential trend reversals.

What are Bollinger Bands and how are they used?

Bollinger Bands are volatility indicators that consist of a moving average and two standard deviation lines (upper and lower bands).

  • Upper Band: Indicates a potential overbought condition when prices touch or exceed it.
  • Lower Band: Indicates a potential oversold condition when prices touch or fall below it.
  • Usage Tip: Narrowing bands may signal upcoming volatility; widening bands often follow high volatility periods.
